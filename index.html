Job History

Teacher

I stumbled into a job as a high school Latin teacher. It turned out to be fun.

After a few years I realized I wasn't learning as much as I had at the beginning. I got restless and decided I needed a Ph.D. in Philosophy. It was obvious.

Philosopher

In graduate school, I learned to write my papers using . This was my first step into coding.

After finishing the Ph.D., I was hired as a post-doc in Oxford. This was a lot of fun: no end of challenge and learning. I published a few papers and gave a few talks.

Then it was time to re-job. After being spoiled in Oxford, I was no longer willing to take temporary jobs. This is typically part of the entry price to the field, and entails moving across the country or around the world every year. I returned to teaching Latin.

Teacher

I found more to learn in the new Latin job, and wonderful colleagues. But my learning again declined over time, and I miss having a challenge. I've been studying web development technologies for the past couple of years, and hope to change careers in 2022.

Web Developer

Coding for the web combines several things I loved about philosophy:

  • Logic
  • Typesetting: made it a pleasure to read what I was writing. Arranging elements on a surface to be attractive and so that information is easy to read is a great pleasure.
  • Git
  • Vim: I wrote my dissertation and research papers in Vim. Among other things, it removes the enormous distractions of trying to watch a program do typesetting badly at the same time as one is trying to focus on the content one is writing.
